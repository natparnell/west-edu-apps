Algebraic Working

Differentiation from first principles finds the gradient of a curve at a point by considering what happens to the gradient of a secant as the two points get closer together.

f′(x) = limh→0   [f(x + h) − f(x)] / h

When h is large, the secant line is a rough approximation. As h shrinks toward zero, the secant gradient converges to the exact gradient of the tangent.

This limit only exists if f is continuous and smooth at x. For polynomial functions the algebra simplifies cleanly; for transcendental functions (sin, ex) the proof requires additional limit results.
